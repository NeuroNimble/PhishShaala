Phishing Basics

Phishing is a deceptive technique used by cybercriminals to trick you into revealing sensitive information. It often involves mimicking legitimate organizations through email or text to steal passwords, financial details, or personal data.

Red Flags Analysis

warning

Generic Greetings

"Dear Customer" or "Valued Member" often indicates a mass blast rather than a personal communication.

timer

Artificial Urgency

Phrases like "Action Required Immediately" or "Your account will be suspended in 24h" trigger panic.

Prevention Tips

  • check_circle Verify the sender's actual email address, not just the display name.
  • check_circle Hover over links to preview the URL before clicking.
  • check_circle Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all accounts.

Red Flags Analysis

link_off

Shortened URLs

Use of bit.ly or tinyurl links to hide the destination, often leading to credential harvesting sites.

inventory_2

Delivery Rescheduling

"Your package is delayed. Click here to reschedule." This targets the anticipation of deliveries.

Prevention Tips

  • check_circle Never click links in unexpected text messages.
  • check_circle Contact the alleged sender (e.g., DHL, FedEx) through their official app.
  • check_circle Report spam texts to your carrier (usually forward to 7726).

Red Flags Analysis

record_voice_over

Spoofed Numbers

The Caller ID might say "IRS" or "Apple Support," but it's easily faked using VoIP technology.

admin_panel_settings

Request for Remote Access

Legitimate companies will never cold call you and ask to install AnyDesk or TeamViewer.

Prevention Tips

  • check_circle Let unknown numbers go to voicemail.
  • check_circle Hang up and call back using the official number from the back of your card.
  • check_circle Never give out OTPs or passwords over the phone.

school Module 01

Email Phishing

The most common form of cyber attack where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations via email to steal credentials or infect devices.

lightbulb Theory

Email phishing relies on volume and social engineering. Unlike targeted attacks, generic phishing casts a wide net, hoping that a small percentage of recipients will fall for the ruse. These emails often create a false sense of urgency or curiosity.

The attacker's goal is typically one of two things:
1. Credential Harvesting: Tricking you into logging into a fake page.
2. Malware Delivery: Convincing you to download an infected attachment.

settings_suggest How it Works

1

Spoofing

Attacker registers a domain similar to a real one (e.g., paypa1.com vs paypal.com).

2

The Lure

A generic email is sent with a subject line like "Urgent: Account Suspended" or "Invoice #1234 Overdue".

3

The Trap

Victim clicks a link and enters their password on the fake site. The attacker captures it instantly.

bug_report Example Scenario

Subject: Action Required: Unusual Activity
Phishing Attempt

From: PayPal Support <security@paypa1.com>

Date: Today, 10:42 AM

Hello Customer,

We noticed a new login to your account from an unrecognized device in Russia. For your security, we have temporarily limited your account access.

If you do not verify within 24 hours, your account will be permanently suspended.

warning Notice the sender domain is paypa1.com (digit 1 instead of letter l) and the generic "Hello Customer" greeting.

shield Safety Tips

  • check_circle Check the sender's full email address, not just the display name.
  • check_circle Hover over links without clicking to preview the actual URL.
  • check_circle Beware of generic greetings like "Dear Customer".
  • check_circle Verify urgent requests through a secondary channel (e.g., call the bank).
sms Module 02

Smishing

SMS Phishing attacks target users via text messages, often pretending to be delivery services or banks.

lightbulb Theory

Smishing (SMS Phishing) is a form of social engineering that uses text messages to deceive victims. Because text messages feel more personal and urgent than email, users are often quicker to trust them.

Attackers exploit this trust by sending short, alarming messages that demand immediate action, such as verifying a transaction or claiming a package.

settings_suggest How it Works

1

The Setup

You receive a text from a "Delivery Service" claiming a package couldn't be delivered.

2

The Link

The message includes a shortened link (e.g., bit.ly) to "reschedule" the delivery.

3

The Payoff

The link leads to a fake site asking for a small "redelivery fee" via credit card.

bug_report Example Scenario

+1 (555) 019-2834
Your package #TRK8921 is currently held at our distribution warehouse. Please pay the outstanding customs fee of $2.00 to release delivery: bit.ly/fake-link
12:04 PM

warning The message creates urgency over a trivial amount ($2) and uses a shortened URL to hide the destination.

shield Safety Tips

  • check_circle Never click links in unsolicited text messages.
  • check_circle Verify the sender's short code or number online.
  • check_circle Contact the company directly using their official website.
  • check_circle Block numbers that send suspicious spam texts.
call Module 03

Vishing

Voice Phishing involves attackers making phone calls to deceive victims into revealing personal information.

lightbulb Theory

Vishing (Voice Phishing) relies on voice manipulation and social engineering over the phone. Attackers often use Caller ID spoofing to make calls appear to come from a trusted source, like a bank or government agency.

Modern vishing attacks may even use AI-generated voices to mimic executives or family members in distress.

settings_suggest How it Works

1

The Robocall

Automated systems dial thousands of numbers, playing a pre-recorded threat (e.g., "Your social security number is suspended").

2

The Escalation

The victim presses '1' to speak to an "agent," who is actually a fraudster in a call center.

3

AI Manipulation

In advanced attacks, AI voice skins are used to impersonate a specific person's voice to authorize transfers.

bug_report Example Scenario

phone_in_talk

Incoming Call: "Tax Support"

+1 (800) 555-TAX-HELP

Agent: "This is Officer Smith from Tax Support. We have a warrant for your arrest due to unpaid back taxes."

Agent: "To avoid immediate jail time, you must pay the penalty of $500 right now."

Agent: "We only accept payment via Target or Apple Gift Cards. Do not hang up or the police will be dispatched."

shield Safety Tips

  • check_circle Verify the caller by hanging up and calling the official number.
  • check_circle Never give out sensitive info (PINs, passwords) over the phone.
  • check_circle Be skeptical of unknown numbers, even if the area code looks local.
  • check_circle Let unknown calls go to voicemail to screen them first.
alternate_email Module 04

Social Media Phishing

Attacks executed on social platforms like Facebook, LinkedIn, or Twitter, leveraging personal connections.

lightbulb Theory

Social media phishing often involves profile cloning or hijacking. Attackers create a duplicate profile of someone you know, using their photos and details, to trick you into lowering your guard.

Once connected, they may send malicious links via Direct Message (DM) or ask for money, claiming an emergency.

settings_suggest How it Works

1

Profile Cloning

The attacker copies a target's profile picture and bio, creating a nearly identical account.

2

The Friend Request

They send friend requests to the target's friends, who often accept thinking it's the real person.

3

Urgent DMs

The fake account sends a message: "I'm stuck in London, lost my wallet, can you wire me cash?"

bug_report Example Scenario

person

Sarah Jenkins (New Account)

Active 2m ago

Hey!! I got locked out of my main account Instagram sent a reset code to your phone by accident because you're in my trusted contacts.
Can you please screenshot it and send it to me so I can log back in?? Pls hurry!

warning This is a takeover scam. If you send the code, the attacker will use it to take over YOUR account.

shield Safety Tips

  • check_circle Check profile age and follower count before accepting requests.
  • check_circle Contact your friend through another channel (text/call) to verify.
  • check_circle Report duplicate profiles to the platform immediately.
  • check_circle Limit public visibility of your friends list to prevent targeting.
corporate_fare Module 05

Whaling

Targeted phishing attacks aimed at senior executives or high-profile individuals within an organization.

lightbulb Theory

Whaling is a specific form of spear phishing that targets the "big fish" CEOs, CFOs, or other high-value targets. The goal is often to steal sensitive company data or initiate large fraudulent wire transfers.

These attacks are highly researched and sophisticated, often using business language and insider knowledge to appear authentic.

settings_suggest How it Works

1

Reconnaissance

Attackers study the executive's schedule, speaking style, and business partners online.

2

Impersonation

An email is sent appearing to be from the CEO to the finance department, requesting an "urgent vendor payment".

3

Pressure Tactics

The message emphasizes confidentiality and urgency (e.g., "for a secret acquisition") to bypass standard procedures.

bug_report Example Scenario

Subject: Urgent Wire Transfer - Project Alpha

From: John Doe (CEO) <ceo@your-company.co>

To: Finance Controller

Hi,

I'm currently in a meeting with a new vendor for the Alpha acquisition. We need to process an initial deposit of $45,000 immediately to secure the deal.

Please wire the funds to the attached account details within the next 30 minutes. This is highly confidential, so do not discuss it with anyone else until I return.

Sent from my iPhone

warning The sender domain is .co instead of .com. The email demands urgency and secrecy to prevent verification.

shield Safety Tips

  • check_circle Implement multi-channel verification for all large fund transfers.
  • check_circle Train staff to recognize executive impersonation attempts.
  • check_circle Use digital signatures or encrypted email for sensitive communications.
  • check_circle Verify any request that asks to bypass standard security protocols.
content_copy Module 06

Clone Phishing

A technique where a legitimate, previously delivered email is cloned and the content is modified to include malicious links.

lightbulb Theory

Clone phishing takes a legitimate email that the victim has already received and creates an identical copy. The attacker then resends this "cloned" email from a spoofed address that looks like the original sender.

The key difference is that the attachments or links in the email are replaced with malicious versions, while the rest of the content remains exactly the same to maintain trust.

settings_suggest How it Works

1

Interception

The attacker intercepts a legitimate email or obtains one from a previous breach.

2

Modification

They replace the original attachment or link with malware or a phishing site link.

3

Resending

The email is sent with a subject like "Updated Invoice" or "Resending important document".

bug_report Example Scenario

Original Email

From: Microsoft Security

We detected a sign-in attempt.

Review Activity

Link: microsoft.com/security

Cloned Email

From: Microsoft Security <alert@ms-update.net>

We detected a sign-in attempt.

Review Activity

Link: ms-security-verify.net

warning The content is visually identical. The only tells are the slight change in sender address and the malicious link destination.

shield Safety Tips

  • check_circle Inspect link destinations carefully, even in emails that look familiar.
  • check_circle Check the sender's address for subtle misspellings.
  • check_circle Be wary of "updated version" emails sent shortly after the original.
  • check_circle Scan all attachments with antivirus software before opening.
